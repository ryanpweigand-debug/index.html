Two-Part Rubric System. Part A is the existing formal scorecard (used in this cycle's evaluation). Part B is the proposed qualitative rubric — capturing intangibles Dwight observes that the formal rubric cannot score. Both are designed to be scored on a 1–5 scale per category, then weighted to a 100-point total.
PART A — Formal Scorecard Rubric

Seven categories · Weighted to 100 points · Administered by evaluator review of application materials

PART B — Qualitative Intangibles Rubric PROPOSED

Six categories · Weighted to 100 points · Scored by Dwight or senior team member with direct candidate contact

Scoring Authority: Part B scores should only be assigned by someone with direct, in-person or repeated contact with the candidate. These are not application-document scores — they require relationship-based observation.
